A Tribute to Kim Jonghyun:

A Diamond That Will Never Lose Its Shine

A Life of Artistry and Passion

  • 1990: Born on April 8 in Seoul, South Korea.
  • 2008: Debuted as SHINee's main vocalist on May 19 with "Replay (Noona Neomu Yeppeo".
  • 2010: SHINee won their first music show award with "Lucifer."
  • 2012: Composed "Juliette" for SHINee, marking his start as a credited songwriter.
  • 2015: Release solo album Base and topped Billboard World Albums Chart. Also in the same year, won Best Male Artist at Seoul Music Awards (Solo).
  • 2016: Published essay collection Skeleton Flower, was listed as a bestseller for 12 weeks. Critics praised its "unflinching honesty" (Hankook Ilbo) and "poetic vulnerability" (Kookmin Daily) with particular acclaim for breaking idol stereotypes by addressing mental health openly.
  • 2017: Posthumorously awarded Minister of Culture Commendation for cultural contributions for Pioneering artistic authenticity in K-pop through his songwriting (100+ credited songs) and breaking stigma around mental health with lyrics in Lonely (with Taeyeon) and Breathe (for Lee Hi). He sparked the inspiration for national conversation about artist well-being in the midst of the harsh K-pop industry.
  • 2017: The world lost Kim Jonghyun to depression, but his light continues to shine through.
  • 2018: SM Entertainment released his final album. The album Poet | Artist charted and won Album of the Year at Korean Music Awards.
